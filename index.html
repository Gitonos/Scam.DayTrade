Staying Safe from Day Trading Scams

Protect yourself by knowing the red flags and checking with official resources.

Common Red Flags

  • Unrealistic Guarantees: Promises of high returns with little to no risk.
  • Pressure to Act Fast: Urging you to "act now" before an opportunity is gone.
  • Unlicensed Brokers: Check with official regulators like the SEC or FCA.
  • Fake Testimonials: Reviews that seem too good to be true, often with stock photos.
  • Obfuscated Code: Unreadable or hidden JavaScript in the website's source.
  • Inability to Withdraw Funds: Excuses or delays when you try to pull your money out.

Official Takedown & Alert Websites

These organizations are on the front lines, providing warnings and working to shut down fraudulent operations.

  • Financial Conduct Authority (FCA) - UK

    The FCA's ScamSmart and Warning List are excellent resources. They publish alerts about unauthorized firms and investment scams.

  • Australian Securities and Investments Commission (ASIC)

    ASIC's Investor Alert List is regularly updated with entities that are fraudulent or impersonating legitimate businesses.

  • U.S. Securities and Exchange Commission (SEC)

    The SEC's Investor.gov website provides tools and resources to help you check out a professional or firm's background.

  • Federal Trade Commission (FTC)

    The FTC's ReportFraud.ftc.gov is where you can report scams. They also publish consumer alerts and advice.
